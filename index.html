Miss Franny Block

The town librarian. She has been there for decades, surrounded by stories, alone in her building. She tells Opal about a bear that walked into her library when she was young. She told it so many times she might have started to believe it differently than it happened.

What do stories do for someone who is old and mostly alone?
Otis

He plays guitar for the animals in the pet shop. He has a past he doesn't talk about. Opal doesn't ask. There is something in the way DiCamillo writes him — gentle, slightly apart, like someone who got used to being misunderstood.

Why might music be easier than words for some people?
Gloria Dump

Her garden is wild and overgrown. Her eyesight is poor. She has a tree covered in glass bottles — one for each mistake she made. She doesn't hide her mistakes. She puts them somewhere she can see them.

What is the difference between remembering your mistakes and being ashamed of them?
Dunlap & Stevie Dewberry

The boys who live nearby. They tease Opal, keep a cautious distance from Winn-Dixie, and say unkind things about Gloria Dump. It would be easy to call them the villains. DiCamillo doesn't quite let that happen.

What might the Dewberry boys be afraid of?
Amanda Wilkinson

She has a pinched-looking face, Opal thinks. She is formal, careful with books, precise. She is carrying something Opal doesn't know about yet. When Opal finds out, everything shifts.

Have you ever misjudged someone and then had to revise what you thought?
Gertrude the Parrot

She sits in Otis's pet shop and says one word: "Jesus." Nobody knows why. She is small and improbable and she makes Opal laugh. Some presences in a story exist to remind you that the world is stranger and funnier than it seems.

What does humour do in a story full of loneliness?
All of these characters come to Opal's party at the end. They would not have met otherwise.
Winn-Dixie is the thread between them.
What do all of them — even the Preacher, even Amanda — have in common?
If they were all at the same table, what would they understand about each other that no one else would?

Strange & Tender Moments
Mid reading · Zone Four
The emotional core of the unit. These surfaces reward slow class discussion more than individual task completion. Allow silence. The ranking works best after students have finished the book.
"Littmus W. Block had gone off to fight in the Civil War, and when he came home, there was nothing to come home to. He was completely and utterly alone in the world. And so, to deal with his sadness, he built a candy factory."
Because of Winn-Dixie, Chapter 15
The Littmus Lozenge tastes sweet and sad at the same time.
Littmus Block built a candy factory out of grief. What do we build out of ours?
